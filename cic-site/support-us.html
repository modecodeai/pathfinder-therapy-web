Why support matters

Accessible therapy can change the direction of someone’s journey.

When people reach out for help, cost, confusion and availability can become barriers. Pathfinder aims to reduce those barriers by developing accessible therapy pathways, clear referral routes and veteran-aware clinical support.

Your support can help create therapy places, fund community access, develop training, build partnerships and strengthen the infrastructure needed to deliver safe services.

Improve Access

Help people access therapy earlier, before difficulties become more entrenched.

Support Veterans

Help veterans of all genders, serving personnel, families and the wider Armed Forces community access appropriate support.

Build Capacity

Help Pathfinder develop sustainable services, training pathways and responsible clinical governance.

Funding and partnership language

Our purpose is community benefit.

Pathfinder Therapy CIC exists to provide accessible counselling and psychotherapy for veterans of all genders and adults in the wider community. We are developing services that support people navigating trauma, transition, injury, grief, anxiety, depression, identity change and life’s difficult terrain.

We welcome discussions with funders and partners who want to improve access to safe, professional and clinically grounded therapeutic support.

Proudly supporting

Working in the spirit of the Armed Forces community.

Service note

Support does not replace crisis care

Pathfinder is not an emergency or crisis service. If someone is at immediate risk, call 999 or attend the nearest A&E. For urgent mental health support in England, call NHS 111 and select the mental health option.
