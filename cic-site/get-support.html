Your route into support

The Pathfinder Journey

You do not need to have everything figured out before making contact. The first step is simply to find a safe starting point.

The Journey Begins

If therapy feels right, we agree a plan and begin at a pace that feels safe, collaborative and manageable.

Reaching out does not commit you to therapy. It simply opens a conversation about what support may be appropriate.

Professional standards

Pathfinder Therapy CIC is an NCPS Recognised Counselling Service, membership number RCS6035.

Our assessment, safeguarding and clinical governance procedures support safe and appropriate access to therapy.

Assessment and triage

We will check whether Pathfinder is the right service.

Before therapy begins, we will consider suitability, presenting needs, risk, availability, professional boundaries and whether another service may be more appropriate.

1. Initial enquiry

We review basic contact information, enquiry type and the reason support is being sought.

2. Suitability check

We consider whether Pathfinder can offer appropriate support within our current service model.

3. Risk and safeguarding

Where risk or safeguarding concerns are indicated, we may advise urgent, statutory or specialist routes.

4. Contracting

If Pathfinder is appropriate, clear therapy arrangements, boundaries, fees/funding and confidentiality are agreed before work begins.

Who we support

Support for veterans of all genders and adults in the wider community navigating difficult terrain.

Pathfinder Therapy CIC provides specialist counselling and psychotherapy for people facing trauma, anxiety, depression, grief, identity change, life transition, relationship difficulty and the impact of service or injury.

Veterans and serving personnel

Support for trauma, transition, identity, injury, loss and the impact of military life.

Men’s mental health

Grounded support for men navigating pressure, stress, anger, shutdown, relationships and emotional wellbeing.

Community therapy

Accessible counselling and psychotherapy for adults and families who need professional support within reach.

Urgent help

If you are in crisis

Pathfinder is not an emergency or crisis service.

  • If there is immediate danger to life, call 999 or go to your nearest A&E.
  • For urgent mental health help in England, call NHS 111 and select the mental health option.
  • For someone to talk to at any time, call Samaritans free on 116 123.
  • For confidential text support, text SHOUT to 85258.
