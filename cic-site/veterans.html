Built for difficult terrain

Life after service can bring terrain that is hard to explain to people who have not walked it.

Pathfinder Therapy CIC was created to provide safe, professional and accessible therapeutic support for people whose lives have been shaped by military service, trauma, transition, injury or loss.

We support veterans of all genders, including women veterans, male veterans, reservists, serving personnel, families and the wider Armed Forces community.

We do not reduce people to a diagnosis. We work with the person, the story, the context and the route forward.

01

Trauma and PTSD

Support for intrusive memories, hypervigilance, avoidance, emotional shutdown, anxiety, shame and the impact of traumatic experience.

02

Transition and Identity

Support around leaving service, loss of role, change in identity, civilian adjustment and finding direction after military life.

03

Injury and Loss

Therapy for people navigating physical injury, disability, limb loss, grief, anger, self-image and major life change.

04

Relationships and Family

Support for the emotional impact of service on partners, families, communication, connection and belonging.

Urgent help

If you are in crisis

Pathfinder is not an emergency or crisis service.

  • If there is immediate danger to life, call 999 or go to your nearest A&E.
  • For urgent mental health help in England, call NHS 111 and select the mental health option.
  • For someone to talk to at any time, call Samaritans free on 116 123.
  • For confidential text support, text SHOUT to 85258.
