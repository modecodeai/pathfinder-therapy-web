Biography

Gregg’s story

Gregg Stevenson MBE is a former Royal Engineer Commando, Paralympic gold medallist and respected advocate for veterans’ health, rehabilitation and mental wellbeing.

Gregg sustained life-changing injuries while serving in Afghanistan in 2009, resulting in an above-knee and below-knee amputation. Following his medical discharge from the Armed Forces, he continued supporting the military community through work at the Specialist Mobility Rehabilitation Centre in Fulwood and later within Op COURAGE, the NHS veterans’ mental health service.

His work with veterans has included helping develop coping strategies, improving engagement with mental health services and increasing understanding of the wider challenges experienced by members of the Armed Forces community.

Gregg discovered rowing after trialling for the Invictus Games in 2018. Together with rowing partner Lauran Rowles, he won gold in the mixed double sculls at the Paris 2024 Paralympic Games. He is also a world and European champion, a world-record holder and was part of the crew named World Rowing Para Crew of the Year.

In 2026, Gregg became Lancashire County Council’s Armed Forces Covenant Project Manager. In this role, he leads work to strengthen support for veterans, serving personnel and Armed Forces families, improve partnership working and ensure the voices of the Armed Forces community are represented.

As Patron of Pathfinder Therapy CIC, Gregg supports our mission to improve access to compassionate, evidence-informed and trauma-informed psychological care. His military experience, rehabilitation journey, professional work and commitment to improving outcomes for veterans closely reflect the values at the heart of Pathfinder Therapy CIC.

Patron role

Supporting Pathfinder’s public purpose

As Patron, Gregg acts as a public supporter of Pathfinder Therapy CIC’s community purpose. His role helps us raise awareness of the psychological needs of veterans and Armed Forces families while championing informed, compassionate and accessible support.

Pathfinder Therapy CIC: Gregg’s lived experience, service background and commitment to the Armed Forces community closely reflect the values and purpose of Pathfinder Therapy CIC.

Gregg’s Patron role is organisational and public-facing. It does not involve clinical practice, therapy delivery, clinical supervision, safeguarding decision-making, complaints handling or day-to-day operational management at Pathfinder Therapy CIC.

Urgent help

If you are in crisis

Pathfinder is not an emergency or crisis service.

  • If there is immediate danger to life, call 999 or go to your nearest A&E.
  • For urgent mental health help in England, call NHS 111 and select the mental health option.
  • For someone to talk to at any time, call Samaritans free on 116 123.
  • For confidential text support, text SHOUT to 85258.
