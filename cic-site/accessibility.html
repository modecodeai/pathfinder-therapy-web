Accessibility

Accessibility statement

Pathfinder Therapy CIC aims to make its website accessible and usable for as many people as possible, including veterans and community members with sight loss, mobility impairment, neurodivergence, cognitive load, trauma-related concentration difficulties and other access needs.

Website:
pathfindertherapy.org.uk

Our accessibility approach

We aim to align the website with recognised web accessibility principles, including clear structure, keyboard access, readable contrast, meaningful links, alternative text where appropriate and compatibility with assistive technology.

Accessibility support tool

The website includes an accessibility support tool that allows visitors to adjust text size, enable high contrast, underline links, use a more readable font and reduce motion. These controls are intended to support users with low vision, cognitive load, dyslexia, fatigue, trauma-related overwhelm and other access needs.

Screen reader and keyboard access

We are working to ensure core site navigation, headings, links and forms are usable with a keyboard and understandable to screen readers. The site includes a “Skip to main content” link to support keyboard users.

Documents and resources

Pathfinder provides downloadable PDF resources. We aim to make these as readable and structured as possible. If you need a resource in another format, please contact us and we will do our best to provide a suitable alternative.

Language and accessible formats

Pathfinder Therapy CIC currently provides website information in English. If you need information in another language, large print, plain text, audio-readable format or another accessible format, please contact us and we will do our best to support you.

Translation note

Because this website includes mental health, safeguarding and crisis information, we do not rely on automatic translation for clinical or safety-critical content. If translated information is needed, we will aim to use appropriately checked wording wherever possible.

Known limitations

We are still developing the site and may not yet have identified every accessibility issue. Some third-party services, including embedded security or analytics tools, may not be fully controlled by Pathfinder.

Feedback and alternative formats

If you experience difficulty using the website, need information in another format, or have suggestions for improvement, please contact us at hello@pathfindertherapy.org.uk.

Emergency support

Pathfinder is not an emergency or crisis service. If there is immediate danger to life, call 999 or go to your nearest A&E.
